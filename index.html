Early Years (1917-1940)

The Toronto Maple Leafs were founded in 1917 as the Toronto Arenas, becoming one of the original teams in the National Hockey League (NHL). In 1927, they were renamed to the Toronto Maple Leafs by the owner and future Hall of Famer, Harold Ballard. The team quickly became a major force in the NHL, winning their first Stanley Cup in 1918. Over the next several decades, the Leafs would go on to dominate the league, with many players and coaches becoming legends of the game

Great Moments from Early History:

  • First Stanley Cup win in 1918
  • Renamed to Toronto Maple Leafs in 1927
  • Multiple cup wins through the 1930s and 1940s

Modern Era (1940-Present)

The team continued to dominate the NHL in the 1940s and 1950s, winning 11 Stanley Cups between 1942 and 1967, including five in a row from 1947 to 1951. This period is considered the golden age of the Leafs. Ever since the Toronto Maple leafs have become one of the most beloved teams in the NHL, even though their last Stanley Cup victory was in 1967.

Key Moments in Recent History:

  • Stanley Cup victories: 1942, 1945, 1947, 1948, 1949, 1951, 1962, 1963, 1964, 1967
  • Long-standing rivalries with Montreal Canadiens and Boston Bruins
  • Some of the most famous players in NHL history: Wayne Gretzky, Doug Gilmour, and Mats Sundin
